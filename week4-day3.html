WEEK 4 · DAY 3

Dates + Appointments

Today you learn to talk about calendar dates and to make, change, and confirm appointments — all in clean A1 German. This is real survival language.

Today’s Plan (4 Hours)

You already know time (um halb acht). Now you attach it to the calendar. Goal: say when something happens and arrange meetings without freezing.

Targets

  • I can say today / tomorrow / yesterday.
  • I can say the date (spoken A1 style).
  • I can say days of the week and months.
  • I can make and change appointments.
  • I can combine dates + time + verbs.

Structure

  • 60 min — lesson + examples
  • 90 min — drills
  • 45 min — vocab in context
  • 25 min — reading
  • 20 min — speaking
  • 20 min — production + exit

Talking About Dates (A1)

In German, dates are spoken with am (on) + ordinal number. You do not need to master ordinal endings at A1 — recognize and use the pattern.

The basic pattern

am + ordinal + Monat
Am dritten Mai.
Am fünften Juni.
Am zwanzigsten Juli.
Date (numeric)Spoken (A1)
01.05.am ersten Mai
03.05.am dritten Mai
10.06.am zehnten Juni
15.07.am fünfzehnten Juli
21.08.am einundzwanzigsten August
A1 permission: You do NOT need to produce perfect ordinal endings. Understand them and use common ones (ersten, dritten, fünften).

Days of the Week + Months

These combine constantly with dates and appointments.

Days of the week

Montag · Dienstag · Mittwoch · Donnerstag · Freitag · Samstag · Sonntag

Key day phrases

heute (today)
morgen (tomorrow)
gestern (yesterday)
am Montag (on Monday)
nächsten Montag (next Monday)

Months

Januar · Februar · März · April · Mai · Juni · Juli · August · September · Oktober · November · Dezember
Pronunciation tip: Months are capitalized and stressed clearly. Mai is pronounced like “my.”

Appointments (Termine)

This is the core functional language: making, confirming, and changing plans.

Making an appointment

Hast du Zeit?
Wann hast du Zeit?
Ich habe am Montag Zeit.
Ich kann am Dienstag um zwei Uhr.

Confirming

Okay, passt.
Ja, das passt.
Dann sehen wir uns am Freitag.

Changing / cancelling

Ich kann nicht.
Ich habe keine Zeit.
Können wir den Termin ändern?
Vielleicht am Mittwoch?
A1 focus: Short sentences are fine. You don’t need explanations — just clear yes/no/time.

Drills (90 minutes)

Block A — Say the date (30 items)

Convert numeric dates into spoken German.

01.01. · 03.02. · 05.03. · 10.04. · 12.05.
15.06. · 18.07. · 20.08. · 22.09. · 30.10.

Block B — Date + time sentences (24 sentences)

Write full sentences.

am Montag / um zwei Uhr / anfangen
am Freitag / um halb acht / treffen
am dritten Mai / um zehn Uhr / arbeiten

Block C — Appointment Q/A (20 pairs)

Write questions and answers.

Wann hast du Zeit?
Am Dienstag um drei Uhr.
Kannst du am Freitag?
Nein, ich kann nicht.

Block D — Correction (15 lines)

Am Montag ich habe Zeit.
Wann du hast Zeit?
Ich kann am drei Mai.
Am Freitag um halb acht ich komme.
Können wir ändern der Termin?
Corrections
Am Montag habe ich Zeit.
Wann hast du Zeit?
Ich kann am dritten Mai.
Am Freitag um halb acht komme ich.
Können wir den Termin ändern?

Speaking (20 minutes)

Speak out loud. Short and clear.

  1. Ask and answer: Wann hast du Zeit?
  2. Say 5 dates out loud.
  3. Make 3 appointments with time + date.
  4. Cancel one appointment politely.

Production (20 minutes)

Production 1 — My week (10 sentences)

Write about your week. Include: 5 days, 3 times, 2 appointments, 2 negatives.

Production 2 — Dialogue (12 lines)

Two people arrange and change a meeting. Use days, dates, and time.

Exit Check

  • I can say today / tomorrow / yesterday.
  • I can say a date out loud.
  • I can make and change an appointment.
  • I can combine date + time + verb.
A1 truth: If you can arrange a meeting, you’re functional.
