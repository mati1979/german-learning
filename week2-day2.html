Accusative Articles: den / die / das + einen (and lots of practice)

Day 1 introduced the direct object. Today you make the accusative articles so automatic that your brain stops bargaining. The goal is not “knowing a table.” The goal is writing clean sentences fast: V2 intact, object marked correctly.

Today’s Overview

Your mission: choose the correct accusative article without slowing down. You will do this by repeating small patterns at high volume. (German is basically a gym routine, emotionally, unfortunately.)

What you will learn

  • Accusative definite articles: den / die / das (+ plural die)
  • Accusative indefinite articles: einen / eine / ein
  • Subject vs object clarity (don’t mix them)
  • Speed practice sets (accuracy under time pressure)

4‑hour structure

  • 60 min — Mini‑lesson + guided practice sets
  • 90 min — Drills (4 blocks: accuracy → speed)
  • 45 min — Flashcards + recall (articles + nouns)
  • 30 min — Quizzes (timed)
  • 30 min — Read aloud + Q&A (answer in German)
  • 25 min — Production writing
  • 10 min — Exit check + error list

If you get tired: reduce sentence count, not the pattern variety.

Today’s rule: You don’t “guess” articles. You run a checklist:
1) Is it subject or object? → 2) Gender? → 3) Definite or indefinite?

Mini‑Lesson

1) The two jobs articles do

In A1, articles do two practical jobs: (a) gender and (b) case. Today you only care about nominative (subject) vs accusative (direct object).

Key contrast Same noun, different job

Der Kaffee ist gut. (subject)

Ich kaufe den Kaffee. (object)

Same noun, different role → article changes (masculine only).

Core A1 truth: accusative is mostly visible as der → den. If you master that one change, you’ll sound 10× more accurate than your level.

2) Definite articles: nominative vs accusative

Gender Nominative (Subject) Accusative (Object) Micro example
masculine der den Der Mann kauft den Kaffee.
feminine die die Die Frau kauft die Pizza.
neuter das das Das Kind trinkt das Wasser.
plural die die Wir kaufen die Äpfel.

3) Indefinite articles: nominative vs accusative

Same pattern: masculine changes, the others mostly stay stable (for A1 usefulness). Yes: it feels unfair. German is a petty bureaucrat.

Gender Nominative Accusative Micro example
masculine ein einen Ich kaufe einen Apfel.
feminine eine eine Du kaufst eine Pizza.
neuter ein ein Wir trinken ein Wasser. (restaurant)
Fast recall line: der → den and ein → einen (masculine object). Everything else: “looks the same” for now.

4) Where the object sits (so you don’t break V2)

Keep the Week 1 skeleton. You’re just adding an object: Subject – Verb – (Time) – Object – (Extra). And time-first still keeps the verb in position 2.

Subject-first

Ich kaufe den Kaffee.

Wir essen eine Pizza.

Du liest das Buch.

Time-first (V2)

Heute kaufe ich den Kaffee.

Jetzt essen wir die Pizza.

Abends lese ich das Buch.

Practice Sets (guided, then timed)

These are “sentence factories.” You build the same meaning with different nouns so your brain stops translating. Do Set 1 slowly. Sets 2–4 faster. Set 5 is timed.

Set 4 — V2 + article (fix the sentence)

Rewrite each sentence correctly (V2 + correct accusative article).

  1. Heute ich kaufe der Kaffee.
  2. Jetzt wir trinken der Tee.
  3. Morgens ich esse der Apfel.
  4. Abends wir lesen die Buch.

Suggested corrections

Set 5 — Timed sprint (10 minutes)

Set a timer for 10 minutes. Write as many sentences as possible. Rules: each sentence must include (1) a time word, (2) a verb, (3) an object with the correct article. Stop when the timer ends. Count correct sentences only.

Target: 20+ correct sentences in 10 minutes. If you get 12–15, that’s still good — repeat tomorrow and you’ll climb.

Drills (writing + saying aloud)

Drills today are more “gym-style”: short blocks, high focus, then change the focus. This builds automation faster than one giant block.

Drill rule: If you write the wrong article, you must rewrite the sentence correctly 3 times, and then write 2 new sentences with the same noun correctly.

Drill Block A (Definite objects only): 30 sentences

Use only den/die/das. No indefinite articles in this block.

  • Include 18 masculine objects (den)
  • Include 6 neuter (das) + 6 feminine (die)
  • 10 sentences must be time-first (V2)

Drill Block B (Indefinite objects only): 25 sentences

Use only einen/eine/ein. Train ein → einen.

  • Include 15 masculine objects (einen)
  • Include 5 feminine (eine)
  • Include 5 neuter (ein)
  • At least 8 sentences time-first (V2)

Drill Block C (Spot the trap): subject vs object switch

Write 20 pairs. Line 1 uses a noun as a subject. Line 2 uses the same noun as an object. This is the fastest way to lock in der → den.

Model:
Der Kaffee ist gut.
Ich kaufe den Kaffee.
  • Use these nouns across the 20 pairs: Kaffee, Tee, Apfel, Saft, Wasser, Brot, Buch, Pizza, Banane
  • Underline the article in both sentences.

Drill Block D (Mixed + fast): 40 sentences

Mix definite + indefinite, all verbs, all time words. This is your “realistic A1 output” block.

  • 20 sentences time-first (V2)
  • Use 6 different subjects minimum
  • Use at least 12 masculine objects (mix den and einen)

Read Aloud (texts + questions)

Read slowly. Then answer without translating. Your brain will panic for 5 seconds. That’s fine. German is just dramatic.

Text A (definite vs indefinite contrast)

Heute kaufe ich den Kaffee und einen Apfel.
Jetzt trinke ich den Tee.
Morgens esse ich die Banane.
Abends lese ich das Buch.
Oft trinken wir den Saft und ein Wasser.

Questions (answer in German)

  1. Was kauft sie heute?
  2. Kauft sie einen oder den Apfel?
  3. Was trinkt sie jetzt?
  4. Was isst sie morgens?
  5. Was liest sie abends?
  6. Was trinken wir oft?
Sample answers
  1. Sie kauft heute den Kaffee und einen Apfel.
  2. Sie kauft einen Apfel.
  3. Sie trinkt jetzt den Tee.
  4. Sie isst morgens die Banane.
  5. Sie liest abends das Buch.
  6. Wir trinken oft den Saft und ein Wasser.

Text B (subject-object pairs)

Der Tee ist gut. Ich trinke den Tee.
Der Apfel ist gut. Ich esse den Apfel.
Die Pizza ist gut. Wir essen die Pizza.
Das Brot ist gut. Du kaufst das Brot.

Tasks

  • Read it aloud twice.
  • Underline der and den (or ein / einen) where they appear.
  • Rewrite the text with 2 time-first sentences (V2).

Production (you own it now)

You’re aiming for “I can produce German on purpose,” not “I can do exercises.”

Production 1 — “Shopping list story” (10–12 sentences)

Write a mini story of you buying and consuming things. Rules: 5 time-first sentences (V2), 8 objects total, at least 4 masculine objects (mix den and einen).

Allowed verbs: kaufen, essen, trinken, lesen, haben

Allowed objects: Kaffee, Tee, Apfel, Saft, Wasser, Brot, Buch, Pizza, Banane

Tip: keep it boring. Boring = automatic. Automatic = A1 mastery.

Example (do not copy, just compare)

Heute kaufe ich den Kaffee. Jetzt kaufe ich einen Apfel. Morgens trinke ich den Tee. Abends lese ich das Buch. Oft kaufe ich den Saft. Ich trinke das Wasser. Ich esse die Pizza und die Banane. Heute habe ich Zeit.

Production 2 — Error-hunting rewrite

Rewrite the paragraph and fix everything wrong (articles + V2). Don’t add new words.

Heute ich kaufe der Kaffee. Jetzt wir trinken der Tee. Morgens ich esse ein Apfel. Abends ich lese die Buch. Oft wir kaufen den Pizza.
One correct version

Heute kaufe ich den Kaffee. Jetzt trinken wir den Tee. Morgens esse ich einen Apfel. Abends lese ich das Buch. Oft kaufen wir die Pizza.

Production rule: If you freeze, simplify. If you hesitate on the article, run the checklist. Your speed will improve after accuracy is stable.
