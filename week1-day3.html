WEEK 1 · DAY 3

Time-First Sentences & V2 Reinforcement

Today is a “German brain” day: you train yourself to start with time (or another chunk) and still keep the verb in position 2. This is where English habits go to die.

← A1 Hub Day 1 Day 2 Plan (4h) Mini‑Lesson Time Words Drills Quizzes Read Aloud Production Exit Check

Today’s Overview

The goal is not “learning more words.” The goal is **speed and correctness** with one rule: Verb in position 2 (V2) in main sentences, even when something comes first.

What you will master

  • Starting sentences with time expressions
  • Automatic inversion: Heute lerne ich …
  • V2 with sein / haben and regular verbs
  • Daily routine talk (A1 style, clean and simple)
  • Error detection: spotting V2 mistakes quickly

4‑hour structure (recommended)

  • 50–60 min — lesson + noticing + micro practice
  • 90 min — drills (4 blocks, increasing difficulty)
  • 35 min — quizzes + corrections
  • 30 min — read aloud + transformation tasks
  • 20–30 min — production writing
  • 10 min — exit check + notes

Tip: If you’re tired, shorten quizzes, not drills. Drills are the engine.

Daily rule: If you write a time-first sentence, you must physically check “verb is #2” before you move on. Make it annoying now so it becomes automatic later.

Mini‑Lesson

1) “Second word” is a trap

V2 does not mean “the verb is the second word.” It means “the verb is in the second position.” The first position can be one word or a whole chunk.

Position 1 (one chunk)Position 2 (verb)Rest
IchlerneDeutsch.
Heutelerneich Deutsch.
Am Morgentrinkeich Kaffee.
In der Stadtarbeiteich.
Oftleseich.
Think in “chunks”: Choose what comes first (time/place), then verb, then subject. If you can identify chunks, V2 becomes easy.

2) What happens to the subject?

When time comes first, the subject moves behind the verb. That’s the “inversion” (but don’t panic — it’s just a swap).

Ich lerne heute Deutsch. → Heute lerne ich Deutsch.
Wir trinken morgens Kaffee. → Morgens trinken wir Kaffee.
Sie arbeitet jetzt. → Jetzt arbeitet sie.

3) The two most common mistakes (avoid them)

Mistake A — subject stays in front

❌ Heute ich lerne Deutsch.
✅ Heute lerne ich Deutsch.

Mistake B — verb drifts later

❌ Heute ich Deutsch lerne.
✅ Heute lerne ich Deutsch.

4) Your verbs (from Day 2)

We keep the same verbs so your brain practices structure, not new vocab.

  • arbeiten
  • wohnen
  • lernen
  • trinken
  • essen
  • lesen
  • schreiben
  • hören

Quick self-check script (use it constantly)

  1. What is position 1? (time/place chunk)
  2. What is the verb?
  3. Is the verb immediately after position 1?
  4. Then subject, then rest.

Drills (the main meal)

You will write a lot today — but in blocks so it doesn’t feel like punishment. Every block has a single goal. Don’t skip block order.

Drill rule: if you make a V2 mistake, rewrite the corrected sentence three times. This is how you delete the English habit.

Drill Block A — “Find the verb” (noticing)

Underline the verb. Then say the sentence aloud. (10 minutes)

Heute lerne ich Deutsch.
Jetzt trinkst du Kaffee.
Am Morgen arbeitet sie.
Oft wohnen wir hier.
Morgen lese ich ein Buch.
Immer hören sie Musik.
Manchmal schreibe ich.
Am Abend essen wir.
Jetzt habe ich Zeit.
Heute bin ich müde.

Drill Block B — Transform: subject-first → time-first

Rewrite each sentence to start with the time expression in brackets. (15–20 minutes)

  1. Ich lerne Deutsch. (heute)
  2. Du trinkst Kaffee. (am Morgen)
  3. Er arbeitet. (jetzt)
  4. Wir lesen ein Buch. (am Abend)
  5. Sie wohnen hier. (oft)
  6. Ich schreibe. (manchmal)
  7. Wir hören Musik. (immer)
  8. Du isst. (morgen)
  9. Ich habe Zeit. (jetzt)
  10. Sie sind hier. (heute)
Answer key (check after you try)
  1. Heute lerne ich Deutsch.
  2. Am Morgen trinkst du Kaffee.
  3. Jetzt arbeitet er.
  4. Am Abend lesen wir ein Buch.
  5. Oft wohnen sie hier.
  6. Manchmal schreibe ich.
  7. Immer hören wir Musik.
  8. Morgen isst du.
  9. Jetzt habe ich Zeit.
  10. Heute sind sie hier.

Drill Block C — Controlled production: 40 time-first sentences

Write 40 sentences, all time-first. Use this template: [Time] + [Verb] + [Subject] + [Rest]. (This is your “muscle memory” block.)

Template examples:
Heute lerne ich Deutsch.
Am Morgen trinke ich Kaffee.
Oft arbeite ich.
Manchmal lese ich ein Buch.
  • Use at least 6 different time expressions
  • Use at least 6 different subjects (ich/du/er/sie/es/wir/ihr/sie/Sie)
  • Use at least 6 different verbs (from Day 2 + sein/haben)
  • Say each sentence aloud after writing it
Self-check checklist
  • My verb is always immediately after the time chunk
  • I used more than “ich”
  • I used more than “lernen”
  • I included at least 5 sentences with sein or haben

Drill Block D — Mixed pressure: 30 sentences (no labels)

Write 30 sentences. 15 start with the subject, 15 start with a time expression. Do not mark which is which. After writing, go back and highlight every verb. (This tests whether V2 is automatic.)

Optional constraints (to make it harder)
  • Include 8 sentences with wir / ihr
  • Include 8 sentences with sie or Sie
  • Include 10 sentences with objects: Kaffee / Wasser / Buch / Deutsch

Drill Block E — Speed round (5 minutes)

Set a timer for 5 minutes. Write as many correct time-first sentences as you can. Stop when the timer stops. Then circle any sentence you are unsure about and correct it.

Start list (pick fast): heute · jetzt · morgen · am Morgen · am Abend · oft · manchmal · immer

Quizzes (error detection + correction)

Do them quickly. Your job is to become allergic to wrong V2.

Quiz 5 — Self-correction (your own mistakes)

Pick 10 sentences you wrote today. Rewrite them starting with a different time word. If you hesitate, that sentence becomes a “repeat 3 times” sentence.

Read Aloud (and rewire)

Read aloud slowly. Then manipulate. This is how speaking becomes easier later.

Text A — A1 Routine

Heute lerne ich Deutsch.
Am Morgen trinke ich Kaffee und arbeite.
Jetzt bin ich hier.
Oft lese ich ein Buch.
Am Abend esse ich und höre Musik.
Manchmal schreibe ich.
Morgen lerne ich wieder.

Tasks

  • Read it aloud twice.
  • Underline every verb (on paper or mentally).
  • Change ich → wir and rewrite the whole text.
  • Change 3 time expressions (e.g., heute → morgen).
  • Make it negative in 3 sentences using only vocabulary you know (hint: use a contrast with aber if needed).
Sample rewrite (ich → wir) — one possible version
Heute lernen wir Deutsch.
Am Morgen trinken wir Kaffee und arbeiten.
Jetzt sind wir hier.
Oft lesen wir ein Buch.
Am Abend essen wir und hören Musik.
Manchmal schreiben wir.
Morgen lernen wir wieder.

Text B — “V2 torture test”

Read this aloud. If your tongue trips, slow down and repeat.

Am Morgen lerne ich.
Heute lerne ich.
Jetzt lerne ich.
Oft lerne ich.
Manchmal lerne ich.
Am Abend lerne ich.
Morgen lerne ich.
Immer lerne ich.

Then swap the verb (lernen) for arbeiten / lesen / schreiben and do it again.

Production (make it yours)

This is where you stop being a student and become a person who can write German.

Production 1 — “Time-first diary” (12 sentences)

Write 12 sentences about your day. Every sentence must start with a time chunk.

  • Use at least 6 different time expressions.
  • Use at least 6 different verbs.
  • Use at least 4 different subjects.
Checklist
  • All 12 sentences are time-first
  • Verb is always position 2
  • I didn’t only use “ich”

Production 2 — Paragraph (10–12 sentences)

Write a paragraph about a normal day. Keep it A1 simple. Include at least 7 time-first sentences.

Include:

  • Morning routine
  • What you do “now” (today)
  • Evening routine
  • One sentence with wir and one with sie

Rule: If you’re unsure, write simpler — correctness beats variety.

Example (don’t copy; compare)
Am Morgen trinke ich Kaffee. Danach arbeite ich.
Heute lerne ich Deutsch. Jetzt bin ich hier.
Oft lese ich ein Buch. Manchmal schreibe ich.
Am Abend esse ich und höre Musik.
Morgen lerne ich wieder. Immer bin ich motiviert.
Oft lernen wir zusammen. Manchmal arbeiten sie in der Stadt.

Production 3 — Spoken practice (optional, but powerful)

Record yourself reading your paragraph out loud. Then listen once and mark where you hesitated. Rewrite those sentences and read again.

Exit Check (10 minutes)

Don’t stop without proving Day 3 did something.

  • I can write 8 time-first sentences with zero V2 mistakes.
  • I can explain why “Heute ich lerne…” is wrong.
  • I can identify Position 1 as a chunk (not just a word).
  • I can write a 10-sentence routine paragraph using time-first naturally.
  • I read Text A aloud twice and rewrote it as wir.
