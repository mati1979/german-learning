Questions: W‑questions + Yes/No (Verb‑First)

Today is huge for “real German.” You’ll learn the two question engines: W‑questions (question word first) and Yes/No questions (verb first). Your sentence control survives because the verb still obeys the same logic: it goes early and stays stable.

Today’s Overview

This day is about asking and answering without freezing. If you can ask a question, you can control a conversation. (Also: Germans will answer you even if you make mistakes. They might just answer you in English as punishment.)

What you will learn

  • W‑question structure: W‑word + verb + subject + ...
  • Yes/No structure: Verb + subject + ... ?
  • Accusative objects inside questions (still accusative!)
  • Answer patterns (short answers, full answers)
  • Polite Sie basics (minimal, functional)

Today’s rule: You do not “translate questions.” You use templates and swap words. Your goal is speed + stability, not poetic originality.

Mini‑Lesson: question word order (the truth, no fluff)

German questions are a word order game

In a statement, the verb is in position 2 (V2). In a yes/no question, the verb moves to position 1. In a W‑question, the W‑word is position 1, and the verb is position 2. Same idea: the verb comes early. German likes its verbs punctual.

Type Pattern Example
Statement 1st element + verb + subject + ... Heute kaufe ich den Kaffee.
Yes/No question verb + subject + ... ? Kaufst du den Kaffee?
W‑question W‑word + verb + subject + ... ? Was kaufst du?
Important: Accusative does NOT disappear in questions.
Was kaufst du? → Ich kaufe den Kaffee.

Micro skill: short answers vs full answers

Short answer

Ja. / Nein.

Doch. (later with negation; today optional)

Full answer

Ja, ich kaufe den Kaffee.

Nein, ich kaufe den Kaffee nicht. (negation comes Day 4)

Today’s focus: Ask + answer. Tomorrow we add negation properly. You are building the question machine first.

W‑Questions (the core set you actually use)

There are many W‑words. A1 needs a small, high‑frequency set. You will drill them in food/shopping contexts (Week 2 theme) so they become automatic.

The A1 W‑set

W‑wordMeaningWhat it asksExample question
Was what thing / object Was kaufst du?
Wer who person as subject Wer kauft den Kaffee?
Wo where location (static) Wo kaufst du den Kaffee?
Wann when time Wann kaufst du den Kaffee?
Wie how manner / state Wie heißt du?
Wie viel(e) how much / many quantity Wie viel kostet der Kaffee?
Two crucial distinctions:
1) Wer asks for a subject (who does the action).
2) Was often asks for an object (what do you buy/eat/drink).
If you confuse these, your answers get weird fast.

W‑question templates (copy, then swap)

Was + Verb + Subjekt + (Objekt/Extra)?
→ Was kaufst du? / Was trinkt ihr? / Was lesen Sie?

Wo + Verb + Subjekt + Objekt?
→ Wo kaufst du den Kaffee?

Wann + Verb + Subjekt + Objekt?
→ Wann kaufst du den Apfel?

Wer + Verb + Objekt?
→ Wer kauft den Kaffee? (verb still 2nd: W‑word + verb)

Wie heißt du? (fixed chunk, memorize it)
Note: If the W‑word is first, the verb is next. Always. If you put the subject first, you accidentally made a statement.

Yes/No Questions (verb first)

Yes/no questions are the simplest: verb in position 1. The rest of the sentence stays normal: subject then object/time etc.

Pattern

Verb + Subjekt + Objekt + (Time/Extra)?
Kaufst du den Kaffee?
Trinkt ihr das Wasser?
Esst ihr die Pizza?
Lest du das Buch?

Answer patterns

Short

Ja.

Nein.

Full

Ja, ich kaufe den Kaffee.

Nein, ich kaufe den Kaffee nicht. (tomorrow you control nicht vs kein)

Polite form (minimal): Sie = “you” (formal). Verb matches sie (they).
Kaufen Sie den Kaffee? / Was kaufen Sie?
Don’t overthink. Just recognize it when you see it.

Practice Sets (guided → faster)

You will build questions, then answer them. Every time. That’s the whole point.

Set 1 — Turn statements into yes/no questions

Rewrite as a yes/no question (verb first). Then answer: Ja, ...

  1. Du kaufst den Kaffee.
  2. Ihr trinkt das Wasser.
  3. Wir essen die Pizza.
  4. Du liest das Buch.
  5. Sie kaufen den Apfel.
  6. Er trinkt den Tee.
Possible answers
1) Kaufst du den Kaffee? — Ja, ich kaufe den Kaffee.
2) Trinkt ihr das Wasser? — Ja, wir trinken das Wasser.
3) Esst ihr die Pizza? — Ja, wir essen die Pizza. (or: Ja, ihr esst ... depending on subject)
4) Liest du das Buch? — Ja, ich lese das Buch.
5) Kaufen sie den Apfel? — Ja, sie kaufen den Apfel.
6) Trinkt er den Tee? — Ja, er trinkt den Tee.

Note: sentence 3 changes subject if you want strict agreement. Prefer: Esst ihr die Pizza? if subject is ihr. If statement subject is wir, question should be Essen wir die Pizza?.

Set 2 — Build W‑questions from answers

You get the answer. Write the best matching question.

  1. Ich kaufe den Kaffee.
  2. Ich esse den Apfel.
  3. Wir trinken das Wasser.
  4. Ich lese das Buch.
  5. Ich kaufe den Kaffee im Supermarkt.
  6. Ich kaufe den Apfel morgens.
Possible questions
  1. Was kaufst du?
  2. Was isst du?
  3. Was trinkt ihr? / Was trinken wir?
  4. Was liest du?
  5. Wo kaufst du den Kaffee?
  6. Wann kaufst du den Apfel?

Set 3 — Insert the object correctly (accusative still matters)

Fill in the object phrase with the correct article.

  1. Was kaufst du? — Ich kaufe ____ Kaffee.
  2. Was isst du? — Ich esse ____ Apfel.
  3. Was trinkst du? — Ich trinke ____ Wasser.
  4. Was kaufst du? — Ich kaufe ____ Pizza.
  5. Was trinkt er? — Er trinkt ____ Tee.
  6. Was lest ihr? — Wir lesen ____ Buch.
Answers
  1. den
  2. den
  3. das
  4. die
  5. den
  6. das

Set 4 — Mixed: choose W‑word + keep verb position

Write a question that matches the cue. Do NOT translate. Use templates.

  1. You want to know the object: (buy) ?
  2. You want to know the place: (buy coffee) ?
  3. You want to know the time: (eat an apple) ?
  4. You want to know the subject: (buys the tea) ?
  5. You want to know the name (informal) ?
Possible answers
  1. Was kaufst du?
  2. Wo kaufst du den Kaffee?
  3. Wann isst du einen Apfel?
  4. Wer kauft den Tee?
  5. Wie heißt du?

Set 5 — Timed Q&A sprint (12 minutes)

Set a timer for 12 minutes. Create 24 question + answer pairs (aim for 2 per minute). Pattern: 12 yes/no + 12 W‑questions. Alternate them.

Minimum rule: every answer must include an object phrase, and the article must match the noun’s gender (accusative for objects). If you can do this quickly, you are genuinely on track for A1 mastery.

Drills (the automation phase)

These drills look repetitive. That’s the point. You are training reflexes. You will hate me for about 12 minutes and then it gets easier.

Drill rule: Speak every question aloud with correct intonation (question melody). If you can’t say it smoothly, it’s not learned yet.

Drill Block A — Verb‑first (Yes/No): 40 questions

Write 40 yes/no questions. Then answer each with a full sentence. Use at least 5 different subjects: du, ihr, wir, er, sie (+ optional Sie).

  • Use verbs: kaufen, essen, trinken, lesen
  • Use objects: den Kaffee, den Tee, den Apfel, den Saft, das Wasser, das Brot, das Buch, die Pizza, die Banane
  • Include 12 masculine objects with den

Drill Block B — W‑questions (Was): 30 questions

Write 30 Was ...? questions and answer them.

Was kaufst du? — Ich kaufe den Kaffee.
Was trinkt ihr? — Wir trinken das Wasser.
  • 10 with kaufen, 10 with essen, 10 with trinken/lesen
  • Answers must vary: don’t always use ich

Drill Block C — W‑questions (Wo/Wann): 24 questions

Write 12 Wo ...? and 12 Wann ...?. Use chunks: im Supermarkt / im Café / zu Hause and heute/jetzt/morgens/abends/oft. Answer each question.

Drill Block D — Mixed rapid transformation: 30 items

For each statement: (1) make a yes/no question, (2) make a W‑question with was, (3) answer it.

Statement: Du kaufst den Kaffee.
→ Kaufst du den Kaffee?
→ Was kaufst du?
→ Ich kaufe den Kaffee.
  • Do this for 10 statements (3 lines each) = 30 total lines.
  • Underline verb position in your questions (verb should be 1st in yes/no, 2nd in W‑questions).

Quizzes (strict + targeted)

If you miss items here, you drill that exact pattern again. No shame. Precision wins.

Quiz 1 — Choose the correct word order

Pick the correct question for each meaning.

  1. “What do you buy?”
    A) Du kaufst was?    B) Was kaufst du?
  2. “Do you drink the tea?”
    A) Trinkst du den Tee?    B) Du trinkst den Tee?
  3. “Where do you buy the coffee?”
    A) Wo kaufst du den Kaffee?    B) Kaufst du wo den Kaffee?
  4. “Who buys the apple?”
    A) Wer kauft den Apfel?    B) Wer den Apfel kauft?
Answers
  1. B
  2. A
  3. A
  4. A

Quiz 2 — Fill the missing word (W‑word or verb)

Complete each question with the correct missing element.

  1. ____ kaufst du?
  2. ____ kaufst du den Kaffee?
  3. ____ kaufst du den Apfel? (time)
  4. ____ du den Tee? (yes/no)
  5. ____ trinkt den Saft?
One valid set
  1. Was
  2. Wo
  3. Wann
  4. Kaufst / Trinkst
  5. Wer

#4 accepts the correct verb that matches the rest of the sentence.

Quiz 3 — Answer in German (no English)

Write full answers with correct accusative objects.

  1. Was kaufst du?
  2. Trinkst du den Tee?
  3. Wo kaufst du den Kaffee?
  4. Wann isst du den Apfel?
  5. Wer kauft die Pizza?
Sample answers
  1. Ich kaufe den Kaffee.
  2. Ja, ich trinke den Tee.
  3. Ich kaufe den Kaffee im Supermarkt.
  4. Ich esse den Apfel morgens.
  5. Die Frau kauft die Pizza. (example subject)

Read Aloud (mini dialogues)

Read both roles aloud. Then cover the answers and respond from memory.

Dialogue A (shopping)

A: Hallo! Wie heißt du?
B: Ich heiße Matilde.
A: Was kaufst du?
B: Ich kaufe den Kaffee und den Apfel.
A: Wo kaufst du den Kaffee?
B: Ich kaufe den Kaffee im Supermarkt.
A: Kaufst du auch die Pizza?
B: Ja, ich kaufe die Pizza.

Tasks

  • Underline every verb in the questions.
  • Circle every object phrase (accusative).
  • Rewrite 4 lines using Sie (formal), if you want a challenge.

Dialogue B (quick questions)

A: Was trinkst du?
B: Ich trinke den Tee.
A: Trinkst du auch das Wasser?
B: Ja, ich trinke das Wasser.
A: Wann trinkst du den Tee?
B: Ich trinke den Tee morgens.
A: Wo bist du?
B: Ich bin zu Hause.
Note: Wo bist du? uses sein. Same question logic: W‑word + verb + subject.

Production (real output)

Now you generate questions like a human in a shop, not like a grammar worksheet.

Production 1 — Build a mini interview (12 Q&A pairs)

Write 12 question‑answer pairs. Rules: 6 W‑questions + 6 yes/no questions. Use at least 4 different W‑words. Every answer must contain an object phrase with the correct article.

Suggested topics: buying, drinking, eating, reading, where, when.

Tip: you can reuse the same nouns. Your brain needs repetition, not novelty.

Mini example (3 pairs only)
Was kaufst du? — Ich kaufe den Kaffee.
Wo kaufst du den Kaffee? — Ich kaufe den Kaffee im Café.
Trinkst du den Tee? — Ja, ich trinke den Tee.

Production 2 — Repair the broken dialogue

Fix word order + articles. Don’t add new vocabulary.

A: Du kaufst was?
B: Ich kaufe der Kaffee.
A: Wo du kaufst den Kaffee?
B: Ich kaufe den Kaffee im Café.
A: Trinkst du das Tee?
B: Ja, ich trinke das Tee.
One corrected version
A: Was kaufst du?
B: Ich kaufe den Kaffee.
A: Wo kaufst du den Kaffee?
B: Ich kaufe den Kaffee im Café.
A: Trinkst du den Tee?
B: Ja, ich trinke den Tee.
Skill test: if you can do Production 2 smoothly without checking rules, you’re building real A1 automation.
