GERMAN ROADMAP · LEVEL OVERVIEW

A1 Overview (4 Weeks)

The goal of A1 here is sentence control: you leave able to write and speak simple German without freezing. Accuracy improves by repetition and correction, not by memorizing tables first.

What “A1” Means in This Plan

A1 is not “perfect German.” It’s a functional base: correct verb position, usable sentences, and enough vocabulary to handle everyday situations. We prioritize structure → meaning → variety → automation.

Core idea: sentence control before perfection.
If you can produce stable sentences, articles/cases become fixable. If you can’t, they stay random forever.
What A1 is NOT: perfect articles, adjective endings mastery, fluency, fast listening, or complex opinions. Those belong to A2/B1/B2.

The 4-Week Structure

Each week has a single job. Don’t “collect topics.” Build a skill, then stack the next one on top.

WEEK 1Sentence Control

Install the sentence operating system

Verb position first. Pronouns + core verbs. Articles appear inside sentences, not in isolation.

  • V2 rule: verb is position 2 in main clauses
  • Sentence skeleton: Subject – Verb – (Time) – (Object) – (Extra)
  • sein / haben (all persons) + regular verbs endings
  • Pronouns: ich, du, er/sie/es, wir, ihr, sie/Sie
  • Nominative articles: der / die / das in context
  • Predicate adjectives (no endings): ist groß/klein/müde/schön
  • Vocab focus: identity, numbers, basic verbs, core adjectives

End-of-week skill: you can write 6–8 correct sentences in a row without freezing.

WEEK 2Meaning Expansion

Add objects, questions, and negation

Sentences get longer and more useful — without breaking structure.

  • Accusative in context: direct object (what receives the action)
  • Accusative articles: den / die / das / einen
  • nicht vs kein (clear rules + examples)
  • Questions: W-questions + yes/no (verb-first)
  • Accusative prepositions: für, durch, gegen, ohne, um
  • Vocab focus: food/drinks, shopping, places, common objects

End-of-week skill: you can ask/answer basics and negate correctly most of the time.

WEEK 3Structure Variety

Make German sound like German

Modal verbs + separable verbs unlock real expression. Possessives make it personal.

  • Modals: können, müssen, wollen, möchten + infinitive structure
  • Separable verbs: aufstehen, anrufen, einkaufen… + word order
  • Possessives: mein/dein/sein/ihr/unser/euer
  • Gender patterns: recognition strategies (suffix cues, categories)
  • Plural recognition: signals and common patterns (not exhaustive mastery)
  • Vocab focus: routine, time expressions, hobbies, plans

End-of-week skill: you can describe routines, wants, duties, and plans with stable structure.

WEEK 4Automation & Exit

Mix everything until it becomes boring

No new “big grammar.” Mostly integration, speed, and production.

  • Imperatives: Komm!, Hör zu!, Mach das!
  • Time & dates: clock + calendar language
  • Mixed drills daily: short blocks, high variety (no 200-sentence marathons)
  • Reading mini-texts: answer in German (simple)
  • Exit tasks: writing + speaking prompts + mock situations

End-of-week skill: you produce A1 without overthinking. Mistakes happen, but structure holds.

Daily Structure (4 Hours)

Writing-first, but not punishment-first. The point is consistency, not heroics.

  • 60 min — Structure: a small concept + examples + 10–15 guided sentences
  • 90 min — Drills (chunked): 3–4 blocks of 25–40 sentences (each with a micro-focus)
  • 60 min — Vocabulary in context: theme words + your own sentences (not random lists)
  • 30 min — Activation: 15 min listening (with transcript) + 15 min speaking (shadowing/self-talk)

Rule: if output quality drops, reduce volume, not consistency.

A1 Exit Criteria

You “pass” when A1 becomes easy to do, not when it becomes perfect.

  • You can write a 6–8 sentence paragraph about yourself (who you are, where you live, what you do, what you’re learning).
  • You place the verb correctly in main clauses most of the time.
  • You can ask and answer common questions without translating word-by-word.
  • You can handle nominative + accusative in familiar sentences (even with occasional article mistakes).
  • You understand the main idea of slow, clear A1 audio/texts.
  • Most importantly: A1 feels slightly boring. That’s automation.
