A1 · Beginner 4 weeks ~100 hours
Sentence control first, then accuracy. Survival German that sticks.
⌄

Outcome

  • Write & say simple sentences without freezing
  • Handle basic everyday situations (food, shopping, directions)
  • Understand slow, clear German on familiar topics

Core skills (ordered for learning)

  • Sentence control: V2 word order, pronouns, sein/haben
  • Meaning expansion: accusative in context, questions, negation
  • Structure variety: modals, separable verbs, possessives
  • Automation: time/dates, imperatives, mixed production

A2 · Elementary 7–8 weeks ~180 hours
Turn structures into habits: cases, past narration, and simple complexity.
⌄

Outcome

  • Hold routine conversations with less “translation pause”
  • Talk about the past (Perfekt) comfortably
  • Navigate daily admin: appointments, complaints, plans

Core skills

  • Dative case + dative prepositions (aus/bei/mit/nach/seit/von/zu)
  • Two-way prepositions (Acc/Dat) for location vs direction
  • Perfekt (haben/sein) + high-frequency participles
  • weil/dass/wenn clauses (verb-last becomes automatic)
  • Adjective endings (enough for A2, not perfection)

B1 · Intermediate 12–14 weeks ~350 hours
From knowledge to independence: fluency automation under real speed.
⌄

Outcome

  • Handle most everyday situations independently
  • Speak with fewer stalls and more self-correction
  • Understand the gist of news and longer conversations

Core skills

  • All cases reinforced (incl. genitive basics)
  • Präteritum (sein/haben/modals) + Plusquamperfekt
  • Passive voice + common patterns
  • Konjunktiv II (wäre/hätte/würde) + polite requests
  • Relative clauses + connectors (trotzdem/deshalb/außerdem)

Checklist (B1)

B2 · Upper Intermediate 16–20 weeks ~400 hours
Nuance and professional German: register, density, and PiA-ready language.
⌄

Outcome

  • Spontaneous conversation with fewer simplifications
  • Comfortable with complex texts and formal writing
  • Work-ready communication (PiA / bureaucracy / professional tone)

Core skills

  • Konjunktiv I for reported speech
  • Advanced passive + alternatives (man / sich lassen / sein + zu)
  • Nominalization + “dense” written German
  • Participles + modifier structures
  • Register control (formal vs informal) + argumentation
