How to Use This Checkpoint (A1 rules, no drama)

This is a test + learning loop. You will write answers, then immediately correct and rewrite. That correction phase is where the improvement happens.

Time plan (4 hours)

  • 15 min — Setup + quick review (toolbox below)
  • 120 min — Do the checkpoint tasks (Sections A–F)
  • 60 min — Review & rewrite (error hunting + second pass)
  • 25 min — Speaking checkpoint (out loud)

If you have less time: do Sections A, B, D, and F + the review method.

Rules

  • Use only A1 structures (main clauses). No subordinate clauses.
  • Keep modals simple: modal in position 2, infinitive at the end.
  • Keep separables simple: prefix at the end.
  • If you don’t know a word, swap in a known word from the toolbox.
  • Do NOT “invent grammar.” Keep it clean.
Pass condition (A1): Your sentences are understandable and mostly correct, and your word order stays stable even when you add modals/separables/negation/objects.

Self-Scoring Rubric (simple and honest)

Score yourself to diagnose what to drill next week. Don’t chase 100%. Chase stability.

Toolbox (use this for clean A1 output)

Don’t let missing vocab sabotage structure. Use these words, reuse them a lot, and keep sentences short.

Modals

Separable verbs

Reminder: plural article is always die (in nominative/accusative). Masculine accusative possessive is -en (meinen/deinen…).

Checkpoint Test (Sections A–F)

Do all sections. Write your answers. Don’t peek at examples while writing. Then use the review method below.

Section A — Core structure (V2 stability) (25 sentences)

Write 25 main-clause statements. Requirements:

  • At least 15 time-first V2 sentences
  • At least 10 accusative objects
  • At least 6 possessives
  • At least 6 negatives (mix nicht/kein)

Starter examples (do not copy; just to show format)
Heute kaufe ich kein Brot.
Am Abend rufe ich meinen Freund an.
Jetzt suche ich meine Tasche nicht.

Section B — Modals (infinitive at end) (20 sentences)

Write 20 modal sentences. Requirements:

  • Use each modal at least 4 times: können/müssen/wollen/möchten
  • At least 10 sentences are time-first V2
  • At least 8 sentences include a possessive object
  • At least 6 negatives

Self-check question
Did every modal sentence end with an infinitive? If not, rewrite it correctly.

Section C — Separable verbs (prefix at end) (20 sentences)

Write 20 separable-verb sentences. Requirements:

  • Use at least 6 different separable verbs
  • At least 10 time-first V2 sentences
  • At least 8 sentences include an accusative object
  • At least 6 negatives

Common trap reminder
✅ Am Abend rufe ich meinen Freund an.
❌ Am Abend rufe ich an meinen Freund.

Section D — Questions + answers (24 Q/A pairs)

Write 24 question/answer pairs. Requirements:

  • 12 yes/no questions (verb-first)
  • 12 W-questions (mix was/wo/wann/wen/wie)
  • At least 10 pairs use possessives
  • At least 10 answers use negation
  • At least 6 questions use separable verbs
  • At least 6 answers use a modal sentence

Templates (use & vary)
Wo ist meine Tasche? — Sie ist hier/da.
Ist das dein Buch? — Nein, das ist nicht mein Buch.
Wen rufst du an? — Ich rufe meinen Freund an.
Kaufst du heute ein? — Nein, ich kaufe heute nicht ein.
Kannst du mein Handy suchen? — Ja, ich kann dein Handy suchen.

Section E — Plural + agreement (12 sentences)

Write 12 sentences using plural nouns. Requirements:

  • At least 6 sentences with plural as subject (use sind)
  • At least 6 sentences with plural as object (accusative plural = die)
  • At least 6 possessives with plural nouns (meine Bücher)

A1 trap: plural verb agreement: Die Taschen sind… not ist.

Section F — Production scenario (A1 mini-world) (1 text + 1 dialogue)

This is the “can you function?” test. Do both tasks.

F1 — Text: My day (12–14 sentences)

Requirements:

  • At least 5 time-first V2 sentences
  • At least 3 modal sentences
  • At least 3 separable-verb sentences
  • At least 5 accusative objects
  • At least 5 possessives
  • At least 4 negatives (mix nicht/kein)
  • At least 2 questions
  • At least 1 plural sentence

F2 — Dialogue: Lost item + plan (14 lines)

Scenario: you lost your phone/bag at a café and you need to meet someone later. Requirements: 4 questions, 3 possessives, 2 modal sentences, 2 separables, 2 negatives, 1 plural sentence.

Review Method (60 minutes) — how you actually improve from a checkpoint

Do not skip this. Test without review is just “performing.” Review turns it into learning.

Step 1 — Mark structure (15 min)

  • Underline every conjugated verb.
  • Circle every infinitive (modal) and every separable prefix.
  • Box every object phrase (accusative): den/die/das/einen + noun, or possessive object.

Step 2 — Identify mistakes (15 min)

Use this checklist:

  • V2 broken? (verb not in position 2)
  • Modal missing infinitive at end?
  • Separable prefix not at end?
  • Question word order wrong? (verb-first or W + verb)
  • nicht vs kein wrong?
  • Accusative article wrong? (den/einen, etc.)
  • Possessive ending wrong? (esp. masculine accusative -en)
  • Plural agreement wrong? (sind vs ist)

Step 3 — Rewrite correctly (20 min)

Choose the 15 worst sentences and rewrite them correctly. Then rewrite 5 of them again, faster, without looking.

Step 4 — Mini drill (10 min)

Pick your #1 mistake category and do a focused drill: 10 sentences where you force the correct pattern. Example: if your mistake is masculine accusative possessive → write 10 sentences with meinen/deinen.

Checkpoint secret: The rewrite phase is the real training. The test just tells you where to aim.

Fix Plan (for next week) — 10 minutes/day

Based on your rubric scores, pick 1–2 daily fixes. Keep it small and consistent.

If you struggle with…Daily 10-minute fix
V2 word order Write 10 time-first V2 sentences with simple verbs. Underline the verb. Repeat next day.
Modals Write 10 modal sentences. Circle the infinitive at the end. 5 with negation.
Separable verbs Write 10 separable sentences. Circle the prefix at the end.
Accusative articles Write 12 sentences with direct objects: 6 masculine (den/einen), 3 feminine, 3 neuter.
Possessives Write 12 sentences forcing: meinen (6) + meine (3) + mein (3).
nicht vs kein Write 12 pairs: “kein noun” vs “nicht my/your noun” and explain why each uses it.
Plurals Write 10 singular→plural pairs, then 10 sentences with plural subjects using sind.
Keep A1 in mind: Don’t add new grammar to fix grammar. You fix A1 by repeating A1 correctly.

Week 3 Exit Criteria (A1-compatible)

You “pass Week 3” when these are true most of the time (not perfect):

  • I can use modals with infinitive at the end without thinking too hard.
  • I can use 6–8 separable verbs in main clauses (prefix at the end).
  • I can use possessives correctly in nominative and usually in accusative (especially masculine -en).
  • I can ask/answer basic questions and negate with nicht/kein mostly correctly.
  • I can write a short paragraph + a simple dialogue in A1 German.
And the real sign: It starts to feel a bit boring. That’s automation. If it’s still chaotic, you don’t need “more grammar” — you need more clean reps.
