German Learning Roadmap

A long‑term, writing‑driven, realistic path from A1 to B2 — designed for deep understanding, not speed‑running.

Guiding Principles

  • Sentence control before grammatical perfection
  • Heavy writing and correction over passive exposure
  • Automation beats memorization
  • Progress measured by what you can produce, not recognize
  • Each level exits only when it feels boring

A1 · Beginner

Build sentence control first, then refine accuracy. You leave A1 able to write and speak simple German without freezing.

Phase 1 (Week 1): Sentence Control

Learn how German sentences work before worrying about details.

  • Verb position (V2) and sentence skeleton
  • sein / haben in all persons
  • Personal pronouns
  • Nominative articles used in context
  • Predicate adjectives (no endings)

Phase 2 (Week 2): Meaning Expansion

Add objects, questions, and negation without breaking structure.

  • Accusative case in context
  • nicht vs kein
  • W‑questions and yes/no questions
  • Accusative prepositions

Phase 3 (Week 3): Structure Variety

German stops sounding like translated English.

  • Modal verbs + infinitive
  • Separable verbs
  • Possessives
  • Gender patterns & plural recognition

Phase 4 (Week 4): Automation & Exit

Everything mixed daily until it feels easy.

  • Imperatives
  • Telling time and dates
  • Short texts and written responses
  • A1 exit checks (writing & speaking)

A2 · Elementary

Turn structures into habits. You can narrate, explain, and survive daily life.

  • Dative case and two‑way prepositions
  • Perfect tense (past narration)
  • Reflexive verbs
  • Adjective endings (functional, not perfect)
  • Subordinate clauses (weil, dass, wenn)

B1 · Intermediate

From knowledge to independence. You operate in German without constant planning.

  • All four cases solidified
  • Past, future, and conditional systems
  • Passive voice
  • Relative clauses
  • Opinion, argument, narration

B2 · Upper‑Intermediate

Nuance, register, and professional German — PiA‑ready.

  • Konjunktiv I & II
  • Nominalization and academic style
  • Complex sentence architecture
  • Bureaucratic & workplace German
  • Abstract and precise expression
